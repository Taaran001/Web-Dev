The History of Nuclear Science

-The history of nuclear science can be traced back to the 19th century, where scientists began to explore the properties of atoms and their components. From the discovery of radioactivity to the development of nuclear energy and weapons, the period between 1800 and 2020 saw significant advancements in our understanding of the atom and its potential applications.

  • - 1896, the French physicist Henri Becquerel discovered radioactivity.
  • - 1898, Marie and Pierre Curie discovered two new radioactive elements, polonium and radium.
  • - 1913, the English physicist Frederick Soddy proposed the concept of isotopes.
  • - 1919, the New Zealand physicist Ernest Rutherford conducted the first nuclear transmutation.
  • - 1932, the German physicist Otto Hahn and his assistant Fritz Strassmann discovered nuclear fission.
  • - 1934, the Italian physicist Enrico Fermi conducted the first artificial nuclear reaction.
  • - 1896: Discovery of radioactivity by Antoine Becquerel
  • - 1913: Development of the first mass spectrometer by J.J. Thomson
  • - 1932: Discovery of the neutron by James Chadwick
  • - 1938: Discovery of nuclear fission by Otto Hahn, Lise Meitner, and Fritz Strassmann
  • - 1945: First atomic bomb is detonated at Alamogordo, New Mexico
  • - 1952: First hydrogen bomb is detonated by the United States
  • - 1954: First nuclear power plant opens in Obninsk, Russia
  • - 1957: The Shippingport Atomic Power Station, the first full-scale nuclear power plant in the United States, goes online
  • - 1962: Cuban Missile Crisis, a political and military standoff between the United States and the Soviet Union over the installation of Soviet nuclear missiles in Cuba
  • - 1968: Non-Proliferation Treaty signed, aimed at preventing the spread of nuclear weapons
  • - 1970: Nuclear Non-Proliferation Treaty enters into force
  • - 1973: First commercial-scale nuclear power plant goes online in the United States
  • - 1979: Nuclear accident at Three Mile Island in Pennsylvania
  • - 1973: First commercial-scale nuclear power plant goes online in the United States
  • - 1985: Nuclear Suppliers Group is formed to control the export of nuclear materials and technology
  • - 1986: Nuclear accident at Chernobyl in Ukraine
  • - 1995: Comprehensive Nuclear Test Ban Treaty is adopted, banning all nuclear explosions for both military and civilian purposes
  • - 2005: International Atomic Energy Agency approves the construction of the first nuclear fusion reactor
  • - 2011: Nuclear accident at Fukushima in Japan
  • - 2015: Iran nuclear deal is signed, aimed at limiting Iran's nuclear program in exchange for lifting economic sanctions
  • - 2020: United Nations Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons enters into force, banning the development, testing, production, possession, use, and threat of use of nuclear weapons
