What is Nuclear Technology?

Nuclear technology involves harnessing the immense energy stored within the nucleus of atoms. It spans fields like power generation, medicine, industry, agriculture, and scientific research. Nuclear reactions—fission and fusion—release incredible amounts of energy, making them essential for various applications.

While nuclear power plants generate electricity, the technology is equally important in diagnosing and treating diseases, improving industrial processes, and developing new scientific frontiers. Despite its potential, nuclear technology requires responsible management due to associated risks such as radiation exposure and nuclear waste management.

Uses of Nuclear Technology

Nuclear Energy Production: Nuclear power plants generate electricity by using nuclear reactions to heat water and produce steam, which turns turbines to create power.

Nuclear Medicine: Radioactive isotopes are used in medical imaging, such as PET scans, and in the treatment of cancer, offering precise and effective medical solutions.

Industrial Applications: From sterilizing medical tools to detecting leaks in pipelines, nuclear technology enhances precision and safety in industrial processes.
